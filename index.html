Ludum Dare 38

25-04-2017

I just participated in the Ludum Dare, a game jam where thousands of people try to create a fully-functional video game in 48 hours by themselves. You have to write your own code, draw your own art, foley your own sounds, and compose your own music.

My game, Planetary Defense, is a fun little thing where you rotate around a planet and set down towers (the theme was "Small World"). I used the framework SFML, which is very new to me, thus some pretty spaghetti code.

As someone who previously only used full engines like Unity, SFML is very interesting. I've been watching the YouTube series Handmade Hero, which is what convinced me to switch over to a framework. It's definitely more difficult to program in, but it's so freeing to have access to so much stuff from the ground up.

As a side note, the strange description to my game is meant to be a parody of those old Atari games where they would come up with ridiculously bad stories to try and legitimize the awful graphics.

Standout songs: Little Sadie, Ain't No Grave, and Ecstasy
